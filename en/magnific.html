Magnific (formerly Freepik)

Copy · Product Marketing · August 2023 — Present

The challenge: turning Freepik — a stock library born in Málaga — into a creative platform for professionals and teams, able to compete with Higgsfield, Midjourney and Adobe.

One goal, three projects: a new brand, a product for teams and a studio that proves it.

Since I joined Freepik almost three years ago as a copywriter, the company has changed its name, its product, its positioning and its category. I've been inside every one of those changes, putting them into words — and they all pointed to the same place: where any professional can get inspired, create and deliver a finished product without jumping from one tool to another.

Rebranding

From Freepik to Magnific

The first step was the face: making the name say what the company wanted to be. In 2026, Freepik stopped being Freepik. The rebrand to Magnific wasn't just a name change — it was a complete redefinition of what the company is, does and communicates, leaving behind the "free stuff" association to speak to professionals as equals. A project still in progress today, adapting every touchpoint, tone and message to the new brand personality.

I didn't lead the rebrand, but I was an active part of it: I wrote the POV launch video, adapted key communications like Magnific Originals and Freepik Business to the new identity, and co-wrote the announcement to our creative partners. Each piece, an exercise in translating a new brand personality into concrete words, for different audiences.

B2B

Freepik Business

If the brand was the face, Business was the product that made the focus on teams real. Freepik had traditionally been a B2C product, focused on designers and creatives as the end user. Business was, alongside Enterprise, the bet to enter the territory of teams, agencies, startups and production companies that need a shared creative solution.

A change of audience means a change of everything else: tone, messaging, channels, and the way you build trust with someone who isn't buying just for themselves, but for their team. On this project, alongside my squad, I was involved from the campaign brainstorming phase — visual concepts, narrative, key visuals, key messages — to adapting those messages to every medium and funnel stage, from awareness to conversion. For me, it was the project where I learned the most about building a B2B campaign from scratch.

Studio

Magnific Originals

And Originals is the proof: if the world's best professionals create here, it's a professional platform. An in-house studio that co-produces, alongside the best audiovisual creators of the moment, projects made entirely with the Magnific Creative Suite. The first to launch was The Chronicles of Bone, Kavan Cardoza's dark-fantasy universe, with millions of views.

The work that shines here is the creators' — as it should be. My job was to give the program words: I wrote all the copy around Originals, from the landing that explains what it is and who it's for, to the blog posts, emails and notifications we launched it with. The challenge: communicating something that didn't exist before — not at Magnific, and barely in the industry — and making a filmmaker understand, in one landing, why they'd want to co-produce with an AI platform.

Product

AI suite launches

And underneath it all, the tools that hold up the promise. On a platform that integrates the best image, video and audio models on the market, there's something new to communicate almost every week. With my team, I've worked on the launch copy for the suite's tools and features — from the AI Video Generator onwards — in posts, emails and campaigns that have to explain complex technology to professionals in the time a scroll lasts.
